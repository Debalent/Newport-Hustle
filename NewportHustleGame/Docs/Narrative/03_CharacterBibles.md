## 3.1 — MARCUS DEVILLE (Mid-Game Boss)

### Bio
**Full Name:** Marcus Labron DeVille
**Age:** 38
**District:** DeVille District (east Newport)
**Occupation:** Self-proclaimed "Brand Architect," crypto entrepreneur, counterfeit fashion mogul, motivational speaker (unlicensed)

Marcus DeVille is the most ambitious man in a town that didn't ask for ambition. Born and raised in Newport, he left for Atlanta at 19 to "build an empire." He returned at 32 with a leased Cadillac, a box of counterfeit designer belts, and a narrative about being "a visionary ahead of his time." In reality, every business he's started has failed spectacularly — a smoothie bar (food poisoning), a car wash (flooded the parking lot), a clothing line (sued by three brands simultaneously). But Marcus has one genuine superpower: he never, ever stops. His delusion is so complete it circles back around to charisma.

He renamed a six-block stretch of east Newport "The DeVille District" by spray-painting signs. Somehow, it stuck.

### Motivations
- **Legacy:** Marcus doesn't want to be rich — he wants to be REMEMBERED. He wants a Wikipedia page. He wants a statue. He wants Newport to name a street after him. (They won't.)
- **Validation:** Every scheme is really about proving that he's not the failure everyone says he is. His confidence is armor over deep insecurity.
- **Control:** Marcus needs to be the smartest person in every room. When he's not, he changes the subject.

### Fears
- **Irrelevance:** Being forgotten is worse than prison.
- **Exposure:** If people saw past the bravado to the scared kid from Newport underneath, he'd crumble.
- **His mother's disappointment:** She still calls him every Sunday and asks when he's getting "a real job."

### Quirks
- Adjusts his belt buckle constantly. It's the size of a dinner plate and he's proud of it.
- Quotes himself in the third person: "As Marcus DeVille always says..."
- Names his schemes like movie franchises: "DeVille Coin: The Sequel," "Belt Wars: Episode III"
- Genuinely believes he'll be on a Forbes 30 Under 30 list despite being 38
- Has a life-size cardboard cutout of himself in his office for "brand reasons"
- Speaks in motivational poster language: "Vision without execution is just a daydream with better lighting."

### Combat Style
- **Ranged:** Throws gold belt buckles like oversized ninja stars
- **Melee:** Swings a gold-plated walking cane he calls "The Scepter"
- **Evasive:** Marcus doesn't fight fair — he distracts with monologues, uses minions as shields, and will absolutely run if losing
- **Special Move:** "Brand Drop" — hurls a crate of counterfeit belts like a fragmentation grenade

### Visual Style
- **Signature Look:** All-black outfits with gold accessories. Silk shirts unbuttoned one button too many. Belt buckle always visible. Gold watch, gold rings, gold-rimmed sunglasses at night.
- **Vehicle:** Matte-black 1995 Cadillac DeVille with custom gold trim. License plate: "DVLLE"
- **Office:** Gold-framed screenshots of his own tweets. DeVille Coin whiteboard. Life-size cardboard cutout of himself.

### Relationships
- **Ace:** Sees Ace as useful muscle at first, then starts genuinely respecting him, which makes the eventual conflict more complicated. Marcus actually likes Ace — which is why the betrayal hurts.
- **Trix:** Marcus thinks Trix is funny but unreliable. Trix thinks Marcus is a clown with good taste in cars.
- **Big Rico:** Rivals. Rico towed Marcus's car once, and Marcus has never forgiven him. They insult each other through intermediaries.
- **Lexi Vance:** Marcus tries to get Lexi to promote DeVille Coin. She blocks him. He starts a rival podcast.
- **Dr. Fable:** Marcus once attended an Enlightenment Center session and left after 10 minutes, calling it "a pyramid scheme without the pyramid."
- **The Mayor:** Marcus donates to the Mayor's campaigns in exchange for building permits. The relationship is transactional and fragile.

### Boss Fight Phases — "DeVille's Last Ride"

**Phase 1 — Car Chase (Caddy DeVille):**
Marcus leads a convoy through the DeVille District. Player must disable escort vehicles and PIT-maneuver the Caddy. Marcus taunts over the phone the entire time.
- *Health:* 3 PIT-maneuver windows
- *Attacks:* Escort vehicles ram the player. Marcus drops obstacles (belt crates) from the trunk.
- *Environment:* DeVille District streets, tight turns past neon signs

**Phase 2 — Dock Chase (On Foot):**
Marcus crashes at the Riverfront and flees on foot through the docks. Parkour-lite chase through shipping containers, fish market stalls, and boat ramps.
- *Health:* 90-second timer to catch him
- *Obstacles:* Marcus kicks stalls into your path, slams doors, slides under gates
- *Environment:* White River docks, wet surfaces, low lighting

**Phase 3 — Rooftop Showdown:**
Marcus stands on a warehouse roof overlooking the White River. He monologues about loyalty and legacy while hurling gold belt buckles.
- *Health:* 5 buckle-dodge cycles + final takedown
- *Attacks:* Belt buckle projectiles (3-throw combos), cane melee if player gets close
- *Special:* At 50% health, Marcus removes his jacket and fights more aggressively
- *Finish:* Player closes distance and tackles Marcus. Cutscene triggers.

### Post-Fight Outcome Variations

**Option A — "Turn him in":**
Marcus is arrested. He waves at the gathering crowd like he's being escorted to a premiere. Six months later, he starts a podcast from county jail called "DeVille Unfiltered." It gets 12 listeners. He considers this "viral."

**Option B — "Let him go":**
Marcus drives off in a borrowed F-150. He texts Ace from Memphis: "I'll be back. The DeVille brand is forever." He opens a belt store in Beale Street. It lasts four months.

**Option C — "Offer him a job":**
Marcus works the cash register at the barbershop. He hates it. He's also weirdly good at it. Within a month, he's rebranded the tip jar as "The DeVille Appreciation Fund." Customers love him. He's found his place. Sort of.

## 3.2 — LEXI "THE ALGORITHM" VANCE

### Bio
**Full Name:** Alexis Renee Vance
**Age:** 26
**District:** Operates from a streaming compound near Downtown
**Occupation:** Content creator, "cultural architect," self-described "the Algorithm"

Lexi grew up in Newport, got a scholarship to UA Little Rock for communications, and dropped out sophomore year when a lip-sync video hit 2 million views. She returned to Newport not out of nostalgia but because the cost of living let her build a studio compound for the price of a Los Angeles storage unit. She runs a media empire from a converted warehouse — 12 monitors, ring lights in every room, three editing interns who work for "exposure."

She is legitimately talented. Her content is sharp, her timing is impeccable, and her audience is loyal. But she's also consumed by metrics. Every human interaction is filtered through "will this get engagement?" She once broke up with someone over text because "the screenshot was more compelling."

### Personality
- Speaks in analytics: "That line had great CPE — cost per emotion."
- Genuinely smart and funny, but views vulnerability as a "brand risk"
- Competitive to the point of self-destruction
- Deep down, lonely — the algorithm doesn't hug back

### Combat Style
- **Tech-based:** Deploys camera drones as weapons and surveillance
- **Psychological:** Uses social media to turn crowds against the player
- **Defensive:** Rarely fights directly — uses followers, drones, and information warfare
- **Special Move:** "Cancel Culture" — floods the player's phone with notifications, temporarily slowing controls

### Minions
- **Content Security Team:** 4 camera-wielding guards who document everything
- **Drone Fleet:** Autonomous camera drones repurposed for combat
- **Intern Army:** Unpaid editors who block exits and throw ring lights
- **The Bot Farm:** AI-generated followers that overwhelm digital encounters

### Mission Arc
1. **Viral Warfare** (Mission 08): Meet Lexi. Destroy her rival's bot farm.
2. **Bot Swarm** (Mission 12): Track and destroy an AI clone of Lexi.
3. **Streamline Showdown** (Mission 16): Boss fight at LexxCon.

### Boss Fight Phases — "Streamline Showdown"

**Phase 1 — Social Media Attack:**
Rival floods screens with fake posts. Rapid-fire identification mini-game.
- 60 seconds, 80% accuracy required
- Mistakes flood the crowd loyalty meter against you

**Phase 2 — Drone Chase:**
8 camera drones deployed. Turret-style shooting with T-shirt cannon.
- 2 waves of 4 drones each
- Drones shock on contact if not destroyed

**Phase 3 — Verbal Showdown:**
Face-to-face with the rival influencer. Dialogue options shift crowd loyalty.
- 5 dialogue exchanges
- Each response: +10 or -10 crowd loyalty
- Win at 60%+ loyalty = victory

### Defeat Dialogue
- **Standard:** "Fine. You win. But I'm going to post about this, and the narrative will be MINE."
- **High Respect:** "I hate that I respect you. That's going to cost me followers."
- **Low Respect:** "This isn't over. My engagement will survive this. I WILL trend."

## 3.3 — BIG RICO "THE REPO KING"

### Bio
**Full Name:** Ricardo "Big Rico" Fontaine
**Age:** 52
**District:** Industrial lot, South Newport
**Occupation:** Tow-truck magnate, "mobility entrepreneur," parking lot warlord

Big Rico started with one tow truck and a dream in 1998. By 2010, he owned twelve parking lots, four garages, and the city's only 24-hour tow service. He didn't get there legally — his city contracts were forged, his lot boundaries are "creative," and his tow-away zones appear overnight like mushrooms after rain.

Rico is old-school Newport. He doesn't understand the internet, doesn't trust anyone under 40, and solves every problem with a wrench or a phone call. He's not evil — he genuinely believes he provides a public service. The fact that the public hates him is, in his mind, proof that they don't understand business.

### Personality
- Blunt, loud, takes up every room he's in (physically and vocally)
- Names his tools. His favorite wrench is "Deborah."
- Loyal to people who earn it; merciless to people who don't
- Secretly writes poetry about trucks. It's terrible. He doesn't share it.
- Has a soft spot for stray dogs. His compound has seven.

### Combat Style
- **Brute Force:** Rico fights with raw power. Wrench swings, shoulder charges, grabbing and throwing.
- **Vehicle Combat:** Rico's custom monster tow truck is his ultimate weapon.
- **Slow but devastating:** Rico telegraphs his attacks but they hit HARD.
- **Special Move:** "Repo Rush" — charges with Deborah overhead like a battle axe.

### Minions
- **Tow-Truck Drivers:** Loyal crew of 6 who drive aggressively and block roads.
- **Lot Rats:** Small-time thugs who watch Rico's parking lots.
- **Deborah:** Yes, the wrench gets its own entry. It's practically a character.

### Mission Arc
1. **Tow-Truck Takedown** (Mission 09): Someone's sabotaging Rico's fleet.
2. **Industrial Sabotage** (Mission 14): Rico's forged contracts are exposed. He blames Ace.
3. **Repo Rampage** (Mission 17): Rico snaps, and his tow-truck convoy rampages through Newport.

### Boss Fight Phases — "Repo Rampage"

**Phase 1 — Convoy Chase:**
Chase through residential Newport. Disable 4 rogue tow trucks by ramming their hitches.
- Rico commands over radio with increasing rage
- T-boned obstacles, sharp turns, civilian traffic

**Phase 2 — Monster Truck Showdown:**
Rico's custom monster tow truck in the impound lot.
- 3 charge-and-ram cycles. Dodge and counterattack walls.
- Rico's truck has a hook that swings like a wrecking ball.

**Phase 3 — On-Foot Brawl:**
Rico with Deborah. Dodge-and-counter melee.
- 5 counter windows. Rico swings wide, player ducks and strikes.
- At 25% health, Rico throws Deborah and fights with fists. Faster but less powerful.

### Defeat Dialogue
- **Standard:** "Deborah... I'm sorry I failed you." *(talking to the wrench)*
- **High Respect:** "You drive a hard bargain, kid. Literally. You drove into my truck."
- **Low Respect:** "This ain't over. Nobody takes my lot. NOBODY."

## 3.4 — DR. FABLE

### Bio
**Full Name:** Dr. Franklin "Fable" Abernathy (the "Dr." is from an online certificate)
**Age:** 54
**District:** Spa District / The Enlightenment Center
**Occupation:** Wellness guru, supplement entrepreneur, amateur philosopher, unlicensed everything

Dr. Fable was a high school chemistry teacher in Paragould who had a "spiritual awakening" during a bathroom renovation in 2016. He claims he was struck by "a bolt of pure awareness" while installing a shower head. More likely, he slipped and hit his head. Regardless, he quit teaching, moved to Newport, and opened The Enlightenment Center — a wellness facility that charges $199/month for "spiritual transformation."

The transformation is mostly lavender water, guided breathing, and supplements that are 80% cornstarch. But Dr. Fable is a rare thing: a con artist who believes his own con. He genuinely thinks he's helping people. The fact that he's also getting rich is, in his mind, "the universe's way of saying thank you."

### Personality
- Speaks exclusively in riddles, metaphors, and marketing slogans
- Genuinely calm and peaceful... until cornered, at which point the mask slips and the scared chemistry teacher from Paragould emerges
- Quotes himself as if referencing ancient wisdom: "As the ancients say... actually, I said it last Tuesday."
- Absolutely terrified of actual doctors
- Wears silk robes and one sandal (the other is always missing)

### Combat Style
- **Evasive and indirect:** Dr. Fable doesn't fight — he manipulates others into fighting for him.
- **Chemical weapons (lite):** Throws incense bombs, essential oil smoke grenades, and supplement pills that slip underfoot.
- **PA System Control:** Uses speakers to rally followers against the player.
- **Special Move:** "Enlightenment Burst" — flash-bang of concentrated essential oil mist, temporarily blinds.

### Minions
- **Wellness Enforcers:** 6 yoga-buff acolytes in white robes. Surprisingly tough.
- **Brainwashed Members:** Non-lethal dialogue encounters. They're victims, not villains.
- **Chad:** The ex-member turned whistleblower. May become an ally or enemy depending on choices.
- **The PA System:** Acts as a "minion" — Fable broadcasts manipulation through the building.

### Mission Arc
1. **Wellness Gone Wrong** (Mission 10): Meet Fable. Investigate supplement claims.
2. **Supplement Sabotage** (Mission 15): Scandal goes public. Help or hinder Fable's pivot.
3. **The Enlightenment Extraction** (Mission 18): Fable barricades himself with followers. Boss fight.

### Boss Fight Phases — "The Enlightenment Extraction"

**Phase 1 — Incense Maze:**
Navigate smoke-filled Enlightenment Center corridors. Acolytes throw chakra stones.
- 3 rooms, increasing enemy density
- Smoke reduces visibility; use sound cues

**Phase 2 — PA System Manipulation:**
Fable broadcasts propaganda. Free trapped members through dialogue at 3 stations.
- Each station: 2 correct dialogue responses
- Wrong answers send members after you

**Phase 3 — Rooftop Confrontation:**
Fable tries to escape via rented news helicopter. QTE sequence and final dialogue.
- Pull Fable from helicopter ladder
- He surrenders while giving a TED talk
- Final choice: arrest, forgive, or recruit

### Defeat Dialogue
- **Standard:** "I just wanted people to feel better. Is that so wrong? ...Also the profit margins were incredible."
- **High Respect:** "Perhaps you are the real teacher. I'm going back to Paragould."
- **Low Respect:** "This isn't the end. It's merely... a transition of energies."

## 3.5 — THE MAYOR

### Bio
**Full Name:** Mayor Harold T. Bucksworth III
**Age:** 63
**District:** City Hall / Downtown (controls all of Newport officially)
**Occupation:** Mayor of Newport (4 terms), real estate developer, power broker

Harold Bucksworth has been the Mayor of Newport for 16 years. He ran originally on a platform of "Newport Pride" and has held power through a combination of genuine civic work, strategic favors, and a network of backroom deals that would make a mob boss blush. He's the most dangerous person in Newport because he doesn't look dangerous. He looks like a bank commercial.

The Mayor built Newport's modern infrastructure — the renovated Main Street, the Spa District, the Riverfront walk. He also skimmed from every project, redirected funds to personal investments, and ensured that every business in town owed him something. He's not a crime boss; he's a politician, which in Newport is basically the same thing with better suits.

### Personality
- Every sentence sounds like a campaign speech, even ordering coffee
- Smile that never reaches his eyes
- Uses "thank you for your service" as both greeting and threat
- Keeps a detailed mental ledger of every favor owed to him
- Genuinely loves Newport — in the way a farmer loves his livestock
- Plays golf. He cheats. Nobody calls him on it.

### Combat Style
- **Indirect:** The Mayor never fights directly during normal encounters. He sends security, lawyers, and bureaucratic obstacles.
- **Boss fight:** Uses City Hall traps, secret passages, and finally a gold-plated gavel as a weapon.
- **Political:** His real combat is in the dialogue phase — he's the hardest boss to beat verbally.
- **Special Move:** "Executive Order" — calls in a wave of private security reinforcements.

### Minions
- **Private Security:** 8 professional guards in dark suits. Best-trained enemies in the game.
- **Unmarked Crown Vics:** 3 pursuit vehicles for chase sequences.
- **Voter Intimidation Squads:** 3 groups of 2 thugs at polling stations.
- **City Hall Traps:** Pressure plates, locked doors, camera systems throughout the building.

### Mission Arc
1. **Political Fixer** (Mission 11): The Mayor hires Ace to steal opposition files.
2. **City Hall Siege** (Mission 20): Gather evidence against the Mayor.
3. **The Final Vote** (Mission 21): Election Day boss fight.

### Boss Fight Phases — "The Final Vote"

**Phase 1 — City Hall Chase:**
The Mayor retreats through City Hall's secret passages. Navigate traps.
- 3 trap sequences (pressure plates, locking doors, security cameras)
- Security guards ambush at checkpoints

**Phase 2 — Rooftop Speech:**
The Mayor attempts to sway the crowd from the City Hall balcony.
- 5 timed evidence-counter prompts
- Each correct counter: +20% crowd loyalty to player
- Miss a prompt: Mayor gains +15% crowd loyalty
- Win at 60%+ crowd loyalty

**Phase 3 — Courtroom Brawl:**
Gold-plated gavel combat in the City Hall courtroom.
- 4 hit cycles. Dodge gavel overheads, strike during recovery.
- The Mayor uses the environment: throws law books, flips tables, swings the gavel from the bench.
- At 25% health: the Mayor removes his jacket and tie, fights more desperately.

### Defeat Dialogue
- **Standard:** "You think getting rid of me fixes Newport? I AM Newport. Every contract, every deal, every permit — my fingerprints."
- **High Respect:** "You'll make a fine leader. Just remember — this town will eat you alive if you let it."
- **Low Respect:** "This isn't democracy. This is a coup. And coups get reversed."

## 3.6 — SUPPORTING CHARACTERS (Quick Bibles)

### ACE (Player Character)
- **Default Name:** Ace (customizable)
- **Age:** Early 20s
- **Background:** Grew up in Newport, left to chase bigger dreams, came back broke but determined
- **Personality:** Adaptive — shaped by player choices. Default: sharp, resilient, dry humor
- **Driving Motivation:** Prove to himself (and Newport) that he's worth something

### TRIX
- **Full Name:** Tristan "Trix" Beaumont
- **Age:** Early 20s
- **Role:** Best friend, sidekick, chaos agent
- **Personality:** Fast-talking, scheming, loyal despite everything, drives an '84 Cutlass that shouldn't still run
- **Signature Line:** "Honest work is what people do while they wait for the real opportunity."

### MENTOR
- **Role:** Ace's old mentor, Newport OG
- **Personality:** Wise, tired, patient, sees Ace's potential
- **Arc:** Guide → Supporter → Advisor → Proud Elder
- **Signature Line:** "Newport has problems. That IS the opportunity."

### THOMPSON
- **Full Name:** Earl Thompson
- **Role:** Head barber, Barber's Row patriarch
- **Age:** 70s
- **Personality:** Stern, fair, dry wit, hands steady as stone
- **Signature Line:** "You mess up a cut in my shop, the whole town knows before the hair hits the floor."

### MISS PEARL
- **Role:** Flea market boss, commerce queen
- **Age:** 60s
- **Personality:** Sharp-tongued, generous underneath, runs the market like a benevolent dictator
- **Signature Line:** "That's COMMERCE, baby."

### MRS. HENDERSON
- **Role:** World's toughest barbershop customer
- **Age:** 70s
- **Personality:** Impossible to impress, uses Yelp as a weapon
- **Signature Line:** "I just learned what Yelp is." *(This is a threat.)*

### MS. MABLE
- **Role:** Neighborhood elder, gossip network hub
- **Age:** 70s+
- **Personality:** Blunt, sees everything, comments on everything
- **Signature Line:** "He's still too skinny."
