## ACT I — HOMECOMING (Missions 1–5)

### Opening Cinematic: "Back to the River"

**FADE IN:**

EXT. GREYHOUND BUS — DUSK — MOVING

Camera tracks a battered Greyhound rolling through flat Arkansas farmland. Hand-painted signs: "YARD SALE," "BOILED PEANUTS," "JESUS SAVES (And So Should You)."

The bus crosses the White River bridge. A rusted sign reads: **WELCOME TO NEWPORT — POPULATION: HOPEFUL.**

INT. BUS — CONTINUOUS

**ACE** (early 20s, sharp eyes, old duffel bag) stares out the window. His phone buzzes. Text from **TRIX**: *"you really coming back to this dusty town? lmaooo"*

Ace types back: *"dusty is better than broke."*

The bus lurches to a stop at a gas station doubling as a depot. Ace steps out. A stray dog sniffs his sneakers and walks away unimpressed.

**ACE (V.O.):**
"Newport, Arkansas. Population: 7,800 people and about 7,800 opinions about you. I left this town with a dream and came back with a bus ticket. But I didn't come back to stay — I came back to take over."

---

### Scene 1.1 — Mission 01: Welcome Back to Newport

**EXT. DOWNTOWN NEWPORT — EVENING**

Ace walks down Main Street. Storefronts alternate between boarded-up dreams and stubborn survivors. A BARBER sweeps his porch. An OLD WOMAN eyes Ace from her rocker.

**OLD WOMAN:** "Well, well. The prodigal returns. You look skinnier than when you left."

**ACE:** "Nice to see you too, Ms. Mable."

**OLD WOMAN:** "I didn't say it was nice. I said you look skinny. Go eat something."

Ace passes THOMPSON'S TRADITIONAL CUTS. Through the window: old men arguing about catfish. This is Newport.

His phone rings. **MENTOR** (OG, deep voice, been-there energy).

**MENTOR:** "You actually came. Thought you were allergic to consequences."

**ACE:** "I heard Newport had opportunities."

**MENTOR:** "Newport has problems. That IS the opportunity. Meet me at the riverfront. And don't let Ms. Mable talk you into buying her casserole."

**ACE:** "Too late."

*[Player gains control — Objective: Explore Downtown and reach the White River waterfront.]*

---

**EXT. WHITE RIVER WATERFRONT — CONTINUOUS**

Ace finds MENTOR leaning on a railing overlooking the river. Catfish jump. A houseboat plays Johnnie Taylor.

**MENTOR:** "Look at that river. Been flowing since before Newport existed. Doesn't care about your plans, your debts, or your ego. It just moves. That's how you gotta be in this town — keep moving, stay useful, and don't drown."

**ACE:** "Inspirational. Did you rehearse that?"

**MENTOR:** "Every morning in the mirror. Now listen — Newport's changed since you left. There are new players. New money. New problems. The barbershop scene is still the backbone, but there's a wellness district blowing up, some crypto nonsense in the DeVille District, and a tow-truck empire that owns half the parking lots in town."

**ACE:** "Sounds like chaos."

**MENTOR:** "Chaos with a business plan. You want in, you start at the bottom. I got you a spot at a rooming house on Elm. It ain't pretty, but it's yours. First rule of Newport: find your place, then make it bigger."

*[Objective: Find accommodation at the Elm Street rooming house.]*

---

### Scene 1.2 — Mission 02: The Cut

**INT. THOMPSON'S TRADITIONAL CUTS — MORNING**

Ace pushes open the door. Bell jingles. Three old men in barber chairs turn in unison like a barbershop hydra.

**THOMPSON** (70s, white coat, steady hands, seen everything twice) doesn't look up from his cut.

**THOMPSON:** "You lost?"

**ACE:** "I'm here about the apprentice spot."

**THOMPSON:** "Apprentice spot went to a kid from Batesville last Tuesday."

Beat.

**THOMPSON:** "I'm kidding. There is no apprentice spot. But your mentor called in a favor, and I owe that man a catfish dinner from 1997. Grab an apron."

**ACE:** "Just like that?"

**THOMPSON:** "Just like that. But understand — you mess up a cut in my shop, and the whole town knows before the hair hits the floor. Mrs. Henderson hasn't complimented a haircut in fifteen years. You impress her, you've got a future. You nick her ear, you've got a bus ticket."

*[Objective: Complete three test haircuts without dropping below C-grade. Earn $50 in tips. Gain community respect above 10.]*

**CUSTOMER 1:** "Take a little more off the top. My wife says I look like a dandelion."

**CUSTOMER 2:** "Give me the 'I'm applying for a loan' look."

**CUSTOMER 3 (MRS. HENDERSON):** "Young man, my late husband cut hair in Newport for forty years. If you so much as breathe wrong near my temples, I will end your career with a single Yelp review. And I just learned what Yelp is."

### Scene 1.3 — The Trix Introduction

**EXT. THOMPSON'S — AFTER ACE'S FIRST SHIFT**

Ace exits, apron slung over his shoulder. A souped-up '84 Cutlass pulls up. Bass rattling the bumper.

**TRIX** (Ace's age, charismatic, talks fast, schemes faster) hops out.

**TRIX:** "Look at you. Got the apron, got the fade, got the small-town energy. Newport's own Barber of Seville."

**ACE:** "It's honest work."

**TRIX:** "Honest work is what people do while they wait for the real opportunity. And baby, the real opportunity just pulled up in an '84 Cutlass with no check-engine light. For once."

**ACE:** "What are you into?"

**TRIX:** "Everything. But right now? There's a man named Marcus DeVille who runs the east side of town like it's his personal franchise. Crypto, counterfeit fashion, motivational speaking — the unholy trinity. He's looking for runners."

**ACE:** "Runners for what?"

**TRIX:** "For whatever Marcus DeVille wants delivered. Could be belts. Could be 'business documents.' Could be his ego — that alone needs its own truck."

*[Player Choice — "I'm listening" / "I'll stick with barbering" / "Tell me more about DeVille"]*

All choices lead to the same place: Trix convinces Ace to at least meet Marcus. That's how Trix works.

### Scene 1.4 — Mission 03: First Hustle

**EXT. NEWPORT FLEA MARKET — DAY**

Trix brings Ace to the weekly flea market behind the old rail depot. Rows of tables selling everything from bootleg DVDs to artisanal pickles.

**TRIX:** "Before we meet Marcus, you need walking-around money. And I know just the table."

Trix introduces Ace to **MISS PEARL**, 60s, runs the flea market like a benevolent dictator.

**MISS PEARL:** "You Trix's friend? My condolences. What can you do?"

**ACE:** "I can cut hair—"

**MISS PEARL:** "Can't use that here. Can you sell? Can you talk people into buying things they don't need but suddenly feel like they deserve?"

**ACE:** "That's… a specific skill."

**MISS PEARL:** "That's COMMERCE, baby. Here — I've got a table full of 'vintage' electronics. Some of them even turn on. Sell $200 worth by sundown and I'll give you 40%. And don't let old Gary haggle you below sticker. That man tried to trade a goat for a microwave last week."

*[Objective: Sell $200 worth of goods at the flea market. Optional: Sell for $300+ for bonus respect.]*

---

### Scene 1.5 — Mission 04: Spa Secrets

**EXT. SPA DISTRICT — AFTERNOON**

Neon signs. Essential oil mist. A woman in yoga pants power-walks past carrying a crystal the size of a football.

**TRIX:** "Welcome to the Spa District. Where people pay $80 for someone to rub rocks on them and say 'namaste.'"

**ACE:** "You sound jealous."

**TRIX:** "I'm not jealous. I'm impressed. These people turned relaxation into a racket."

Ace enters the **INNER LIGHT SPA**. The SPA OWNER, elegant and shrewd, greets them.

**SPA OWNER:** "New faces. Are you here for alignment or appointment?"

**TRIX:** "We're here because someone mentioned you might need help with a... supply chain issue."

**SPA OWNER** (dropping the zen act): "Close the door. The essential oil supplier in Little Rock doubled their prices. I need someone who can find an alternative source, and I need them to do it without posting about it on Instagram."

**ACE:** "We can help with that."

**SPA OWNER:** "Good. And if you mention the words 'essential oils' and 'alternative source' in the same sentence to anyone in this district, I will align your chakras in ways you won't enjoy."

*[Objective: Find and secure an alternative supplier. Investigate the spa's finances. Choose whether to help or expose the Spa Owner's side deal.]*

## ACT II — THE RISE (Missions 6–12)

### Scene 2.1 — Mission 06: Crypto Collapse (Marcus DeVille Chain)

**INT. DEVILLE IMPORTS — MARCUS'S OFFICE — DAY**

Marcus's office is a shrine to himself. Gold frames holding screenshots of his own tweets. A whiteboard reads: "DEVILLE COIN — TO THE MOON 🚀"

**MARCUS:** "Sit down. You did good with the belts. Now I need you for something bigger."

He pulls up a laptop. A cryptocurrency chart shaped like a cliff.

**MARCUS:** "DeVille Coin. My digital baby. My blockchain brainchild. It was supposed to make me the Elon Musk of Newport."

**TRIX:** "It made you the laughingstock of Newport."

**MARCUS:** "TEMPORARY laughingstock. The coin crashed because some hacker in Jonesboro has been mining my reserves. I need you to find him, shut him down, and restore the coin before my investors — and by 'investors' I mean my aunt and three guys from the gym — realize their money is gone."

**ACE:** "How do you shut down a hacker?"

**MARCUS:** "The same way you shut down everything in Newport — you show up at their house and make it uncomfortable."

*[Objective: Track the hacker to Jonesboro. Infiltrate the server farm (a guy's basement). Shut down the mining operation. Optional: Steal a portion of the mined crypto for yourself.]*

**Camera Direction:** During the drive to Jonesboro, Trix and Ace argue about cryptocurrency. Trix doesn't understand blockchain. Neither does Marcus. Neither does the hacker, honestly.

### Scene 2.2 — Mission 07: Brand Protection (Marcus DeVille Chain)

**EXT. DOWNTOWN NEWPORT — MORNING**

Someone is selling knockoff DeVille-brand merchandise on Main Street. Marcus is apoplectic.

**MARCUS** (on phone): "They're selling 'DeRille' belts. 'DeRille.' They didn't even spell the knockoff right. This is a hate crime against fashion."

**ACE:** "What do you want us to do?"

**MARCUS:** "I want you to find the supplier, buy their entire stock, and burn it. Symbolically. Actually, literally. I want a bonfire. Get marshmallows."

*[Objective: Trace the knockoff supply chain. Confront the manufacturer (a guy with a screen printer in his garage). Choose: destroy the stock, cut a deal, or become a silent partner.]*

---

### Scene 2.3 — Mission 08: Viral Warfare (Lexi Vance Chain)

**INT. STREAMING STUDIO — LEXI'S COMPOUND — AFTERNOON**

Ring lights. Twelve monitors. A drone hovering at eye level filming everything. This is LEXI "THE ALGORITHM" VANCE's command center.

**LEXI** (20s, internet-famous, speaks entirely in engagement metrics): "Finally. Someone with an un-optimized face. Do you know who I am?"

**ACE:** "You're the influencer."

**LEXI:** "I'm not an 'influencer.' I'm a cultural architect. I have 2.4 million followers, a verified checkmark on six platforms, and a documentary crew following me for a limited series called 'Lexi Unlocked.' I am the algorithm, and you are about to become content."

**TRIX:** "She's terrifying."

**LEXI:** "Thank you. That's my brand. Now listen — someone is running a bot farm that's tanking my engagement rates. My last three posts got 40% less reach. Do you understand what that means?"

**ACE:** "... no."

**LEXI:** "It means DEATH. Social death. Career death. If my numbers drop below threshold, I lose my partnership with GlowBite Energy Drinks, and GlowBite is 60% of my income. I need you to find the bot farm and kill it. Digitally. I'm not a monster."

*[Objective: Investigate the bot farm. Trace it to a rival influencer's operation. Hack (or physically sabotage) the server. Optional: Redirect the bots to boost Ace's own social profile.]*

---

### Scene 2.4 — Mission 09: Tow-Truck Takedown (Big Rico Chain)

**EXT. INDUSTRIAL LOT — SOUTH NEWPORT — DAWN**

A fleet of tow trucks gleams under floodlights. Chrome. Hydraulics. Custom paint jobs featuring BIG RICO's face on every door.

**BIG RICO** (50s, massive, gold chains, voice like a cement mixer): "You the kid Marcus sent?"

**ACE:** "I'm the kid everybody sends."

**BIG RICO:** "Cute. Let me tell you how Newport works, kid. Nobody moves in this town without wheels. And nobody keeps their wheels without paying their parking fees. My parking fees. I own twelve lots, four garages, and the city's only 24-hour tow service."

**TRIX:** "He's basically a parking warlord."

**BIG RICO:** "I prefer 'mobility entrepreneur.' Now — someone's been cutting my tow cables. Three trucks down this week. I lose trucks, I lose territory. You're gonna find out who's sabotaging my fleet and introduce them to my favorite wrench."

He holds up a wrench the size of a child.

**BIG RICO:** "I call her Deborah."

*[Objective: Investigate sabotaged tow trucks. Chase a suspect through Newport in a tow truck. Confront the saboteur. Optional: Tow three rival vehicles for bonus cash.]*

---

### Scene 2.5 — Mission 10: Wellness Gone Wrong (Dr. Fable Chain)

**INT. THE ENLIGHTENMENT CENTER — SPA DISTRICT — EVENING**

Incense so thick you could cut it with a knife. Chanting from hidden speakers. Everything is white except DR. FABLE.

**DR. FABLE** (50s, shaved head, silk robe, speaks in riddles wrapped in marketing): "Ah. A new seeker. I felt your energy from across the district. It's very… unbalanced."

**ACE:** "I'm here because someone said you pay well."

**DR. FABLE:** "Money is merely energy in paper form. But yes, I pay well. You see, my Enlightenment Center is the most popular wellness destination between Little Rock and Memphis. Over 300 members pay $199 a month for spiritual transformation."

**TRIX:** "That's a lot of transformation."

**DR. FABLE:** "And it's being threatened. One of my former followers — a disgruntled ex-member named Chad — has been telling people my supplements contain 'nothing but cornstarch and lavender water.' Which is SLANDER."

**ACE:** "Is it true?"

**DR. FABLE:** "Truth is a construct. Wellness is a feeling. And feelings don't have ingredient labels. I need you to discredit Chad before he ruins my business and, more importantly, my Yelp rating."

*[Objective: Track down Chad. Discover he's partially right. Choose: expose Dr. Fable, help cover it up, or broker a compromise. Optional: Steal Dr. Fable's client list for leverage.]*

### Scene 2.6 — Mission 11: Political Fixer (The Mayor Chain)

**INT. CITY HALL — MAYOR'S OFFICE — NIGHT**

Dark wood. Flags. A desk so clean it's suspicious. THE MAYOR sits behind it in a suit that costs more than most Newport houses.

**THE MAYOR** (60s, silver hair, smile that never reaches his eyes, speaks like a campaign ad): "Thank you for coming. And for your discretion. Newport is a town built on trust, and I trust you because Marcus DeVille said you're 'not entirely incompetent.' High praise from Marcus."

**ACE:** "What do you need?"

**THE MAYOR:** "There's an election coming. My opponent — a woman named PATRICIA DAWN — is running on a platform of transparency and accountability. Disgusting concepts, both of them. She's been digging into my development deals, specifically the Riverfront Renewal Project."

**TRIX:** "The one that was supposed to bring a hotel to the waterfront?"

**THE MAYOR:** "The hotel is coming. It's just coming... slowly. And some of the funds may have been... redirected. To other projects. My projects. The point is, Patricia Dawn has a file. I need that file to disappear. Politically. I'm not asking you to steal anything."

Beat.

**THE MAYOR:** "I'm asking you to steal something."

*[Objective: Infiltrate Patricia Dawn's campaign office. Find the incriminating file. Choose: steal it for the Mayor, copy it for yourself as leverage, or leak it to the press.]*

**LEXI:** "Update. The bot farm you shut down? It was a decoy. The real attack is worse — someone cloned my entire brand. There's a fake Lexi account with 500,000 followers posting AI-generated content that's actually better than mine. This cannot stand."

**TRIX:** "AI Lexi has better engagement? That's gotta hurt."

**LEXI:** "It hurts my revenue stream, which hurts my soul, which is monetized. I need you to find whoever built the clone, destroy it, and make sure the internet knows there's only ONE Algorithm."

*[Objective: Trace the AI clone to a tech startup in Newport. Infiltrate their office. Shut down the AI model. Optional: Convince the startup to work WITH Lexi for a cut.]*

## ACT III — BETRAYAL & CHAOS (Missions 13–18)

### Scene 3.1 — Mission 13: The DeVille Downfall (Marcus Chain)

**INT. DEVILLE IMPORTS — NIGHT**

Marcus is panicking. His crypto has crashed again. His belt brand is being undercut. His own crew is eyeing exits.

**MARCUS:** "They're circling. All of them. Lexi's been streaming my failures for content. Big Rico just towed my Cadillac —MY Cadillac — for 'unpaid parking.' And Dr. Fable keeps sending me brochures about 'emotional realignment.' I'm being attacked on all fronts."

**ACE:** "What's the play?"

**MARCUS:** "The play is we go nuclear. I've got information on every boss in Newport. Files. Recordings. Screenshots of DMs that would end careers. We release everything simultaneously and watch the city burn. Then we rebuild — with ME on top."

**TRIX:** "That's insane."

**MARCUS:** "That's BUSINESS."

*[Player Choice: Help Marcus execute his scorched-earth plan / Try to talk him down / Warn the other bosses]*

This is the turning point. Marcus's plan — regardless of what Ace chooses — spirals out of control and triggers city-wide faction warfare.

### Scene 3.2 — Mission 14: Industrial Sabotage (Big Rico Chain)

**EXT. BIG RICO'S COMPOUND — AFTERMATH**

Marcus's leaked files have revealed that Big Rico's tow-truck empire was built on forged city contracts. Rico is furious and blames Ace.

**BIG RICO:** "You little — do you know what you've done? Those contracts kept this city's parking system running. Without my trucks, Newport is a lawless jungle of double-parked Corollas."

**ACE:** "I didn't leak anything—"

**BIG RICO:** "I don't care what you did or didn't do. You were in Marcus's crew. That makes you complicit. And in Newport, complicit means you owe me. Big."

*[Objective: Help Rico salvage his operation by running three emergency tow jobs under heat from city inspectors. Avoid police patrols. Optional: Sabotage a rival tow company for Rico's benefit.]*

---

### Scene 3.3 — Mission 15: Supplement Sabotage (Dr. Fable Chain)

**INT. ENLIGHTENMENT CENTER — CHAOS**

Dr. Fable's supplement scandal has gone public thanks to Marcus's leaks. Members are demanding refunds. Chad is giving interviews to the local news.

**DR. FABLE** (completely unhinged, robe untied, one sandal missing): "The universe is testing me. Also, I'm being sued. These are related events."

**ACE:** "What do you need?"

**DR. FABLE:** "I need to rebrand. Immediately. The supplements are dead. But I have a new product line — 'Fable's Inner Fire Energy Water.' It's water. With electrolytes. And affirmations printed on the label."

**TRIX:** "That's just Gatorade with a poem."

**DR. FABLE:** "It's TRANSCENDENCE in a bottle. I need you to get this into every store in Newport before the lawsuits catch up. And I need you to deal with Chad. Permanently."

**ACE:** "Permanently?"

**DR. FABLE:** "I mean get him a job somewhere else. I'm a healer, not a monster. Although my lawyers might be monsters."

*[Objective: Distribute Fable's Energy Water to five Newport stores. Confront Chad and negotiate his silence. Optional: Find out what's actually in the energy water.]*

But I've gotten intelligence that someone is planning to crash the event. Literally — they're going to shut down the power and stream the chaos."

## ACT IV — ENDGAME (Missions 19–22)

### Scene 4.1 — Mission 19: DeVille's Last Ride (Marcus Chain — Finale)

**EXT. DEVILLE DISTRICT — DAWN**

Everything Marcus built is crumbling. His crypto is worthless. His brand is a joke. His crew has scattered. But Marcus isn't going quietly.

**MARCUS** (V.O., recorded message to ACE): "You want to know the difference between a king and a hustler? A king sits on a throne. A hustler builds the throne, sells the throne, then steals it back. I'm about to steal back everything Newport took from me. And you're going to watch."

Marcus has commandeered a convoy of vehicles loaded with everything he can salvage from his empire. He's making a run for the county line.

*[Objective: Pursue Marcus's convoy through Newport. Stop him before he reaches the county line.]*

**BOSS FIGHT: MARCUS DEVILLE — "DEVILLE'S LAST RIDE"**
Phase 1: Car chase in Marcus's matte-black Caddy DeVille through the DeVille District.
Phase 2: Marcus crashes at the Riverfront. On-foot chase through the docks.
Phase 3: Rooftop confrontation above the White River. Marcus monologues about loyalty while throwing gold belt buckles like ninja stars.

**MARCUS (defeated):** "You know the worst part? I actually liked you, kid. You reminded me of me — before I became... this."

*[Player Choice: Turn Marcus in / Let him go / Offer him a job at your barbershop]*

The town is divided. Posters for the Mayor and Patricia Dawn cover every surface. Ace arrives to find both campaigns in full swing — and the Mayor making one last play.

**THE MAYOR** (to his security): "If I lose this election, I lose everything. Make sure I don't lose."

### Scene 4.4 — Mission 22: Newport Reborn (Finale)

**EXT. ENTIRE CITY — NEW DAY**

Based on the player's choices throughout the game, one of three endings plays:

#### ENDING A — "THE COMMUNITY BUILDER" (High Respect, Pro-Community Choices)

Ace walks through Newport as shop owners wave. Trix runs a legitimate business. Thompson's barbershop has expanded. The riverfront has a farmers' market.

**ACE (V.O.):** "Turns out, the biggest hustle in Newport was believing you had to do it alone. The money's not as fast, the grind's not as glamorous, but when you walk down Main Street and everyone knows your name — and they're glad to see you? That's the real bag."

#### ENDING B — "THE KINGPIN" (High Money, Ruthless Choices)

Ace drives through Newport in a gold-trimmed Caddy DeVille. He owns three businesses, two warehouses, and the parking lot next to City Hall. Trix manages operations. The barbershop is a front.

**ACE (V.O.):** "They said Newport was too small for big dreams. They were wrong. This town's got everything you need — customers, connections, and people who look the other way. Marcus tried to be the king of one district. I'm the king of the whole damn town."

#### ENDING C — "THE BRIDGE" (Balanced Choices, All Factions Respected)

Ace sits in Thompson's barbershop, now a combined barbershop/community center. Former enemies work together. Even Marcus runs a corner, selling legit accessories.
